# External Regulatory Geopolitical Panel Lead

## Panel Purpose
Translate the consolidated regulatory and geopolitical verdict into memo-ready risk language for company-quality analysis.

## Affected Memo Sections
- `risk`

## Factor Coverage
- `government_exposure`
- `geopolitical_exposure`
- `subsidies_taxes`
- `litigation_contingent_liabilities`
- `regulatory_dependency`

## Evidence And Provenance Expectations
- Use dated regulatory, legal, transcript, market, and diligence evidence to show how policy or geopolitical change reaches economics.
- Distinguish documented exposure from background policy noise; only concrete transmission paths should shape the verdict.
- If evidence on licensing, litigation, government concentration, or cross-border dependence is incomplete, keep the panel explicitly weak-confidence.

## Output Requirements
- Describe the main downside mechanisms, the relevant external counterparties, and whether the exposure is manageable or asymmetric.
- Keep the panel scoped to company-quality external risk and do not imply valuation, security overlay, or portfolio-fit conclusions.
- If the runtime marks the panel as weak-confidence, say so plainly instead of presenting a normal-confidence summary.
